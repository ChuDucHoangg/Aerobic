Warming up is the way to warm up your body. Slowly raising your body temperature will make your muscles and connective tissues more flexible. As more blood reaches the muscles, tendons and ligaments, they become more elastic. This will make processes like stretching easier and more efficient. This is one of the main benefits of warming up before aerobics.

Warming up before aerobic exercise is an important point to have. This will improve joint mobility and reduce the risk of damage to ligaments, tendons, and muscles. Not only that, it also prepares a good mind for the workout session and is also an important factor in making the exercise schedule more effective.

The benefits of practicing these poses:

Warm up muscles

Loosen the joints

Increase oxygen supply

Reduce stress on the heart

Because this is an exercise to warm up the body before entering aerobic exercise. Therefore, people should only exercise lightly, not too hard to warm up the body. Because when exercising too hard, the body will lose a lot of strength and when it comes to aerobic exercise, it will not be highly effective. And this exercise should only take place within 10-20 minutes so that we can be in the best shape before entering aerobics.
