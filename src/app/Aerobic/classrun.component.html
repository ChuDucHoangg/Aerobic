Run & Endurance

Video Tutorials On Run & Endurance

As you'd expect, this type of training is typical of a long-distance runner. The intensity is usually less than 70% of VO2max, or the equivalent of about 80% of maximum heart rate.

The duration must be close to the race distance or at least 30 minutes to 2 hours long. The intensity of slow and long distance endurance training is often assessed with a "talk" test, whereby athletes can hold a conversation without too much difficulty.

Adaptations to this form of aerobic endurance training include improved cardiovascular function and thermoregulation, improved mitochondrial energy production, increased skeletal muscle oxidative capacity, and increased utilization as fat for fuel (removes muscle glycogen). The anaerobic or lactate threshold is also likely to improve as the body is better able to eliminate lactate.

Because long-distance training is low-intensity (lower than competition), over-reliance on this form of endurance running in an athlete's training program can be detrimental.
